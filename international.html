Commitments, Not Officials

The United States makes hundreds of formal commitments to international organizations, treaties, and multilateral forums — funding pledges, emissions targets, defense spending thresholds, humanitarian contributions. Unlike domestic promises tied to a single official, these commitments outlive administrations. This tracker follows each one as a continuous record: what was pledged, what was delivered, and what changed when power did.
